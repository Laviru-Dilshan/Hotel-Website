HEALTH & SAFETY GUIDELINES

At Mahaweli Reach, we look forward to welcoming our guests once again. For the safety and protection of our patrons and our team members we have implemented safety and hygiene protocols in keeping with the standards recommended by the Sri Lankan Government authorities and the World Health Organization.

On Arrival at The Hotel

  • Guests will be greeted with “Ayubowan” the traditional Sri Lankan greeting.
  • Guests will be requested to wash hands at the entrance of the hotel.
  • A temperature check will then take place.
  • Physical distancing is encouraged in all public spaces.
  • We encourage guests to bring their own masks. The hotel will provide a mask on request, which will we charge.
  • Guest luggage will be sanitized prior to entering the lobby.
  • Guests will be seated in the lobby and adjacent areas, in keeping with recommended social distancing

During Check In

  • Guests will be required to fill in the registration card, either in a digital format or on paper. If a pen is used, the pen will be sanitized after each use
  • Guests are required to provide their medical history through a form that will be provided at the hotel.
  • Information regarding, country of origin, flight number and seat number, date of arrival in Sri Lanka, other countries visited in the last three weeks and next destination will be requested for. (This information may have to be shared with local health authorities if so, requested for)
  • In accordance with guidelines provided by the World Health Organization (WHO) and local health & tourism authorities, the hotel reserves the right to refuse hosting any guest displaying symptoms of COVID 19 at the time of screening.
  • The protocols followed by the hotel will be updated to reflect any new standards and procedures introduced by relevant authorities in response to the rapidly changing circumstances related to COVID 19.
  • The use of online fund transfers or credit/debit cards is encouraged in order to minimize the handling of cash.
  • Keys are disinfected prior to issuance.

When Riding the Elevator

  • Four guests are permitted to use the elevator at a given time.
  • The rule of thumb should be used in maintaining the required physical distance.
  • Hand sanitizers are available at all landings so that hands can be sanitized prior to guests entering their rooms
  • The elevator is disinfected regularly.
When Using Public Areas
Spa
  • Spa services will only be available to in-house guests and after same is permitted by the authorities. The spa is currently non-operational.
  • There will be some limitations to the offerings.
  • Guests will be required to sign a disclaimer in keeping with Government and Ministry of Health regulations prior to obtaining services.
Pool
  • The use of the pool will be available to in-house guests only and there will be a limit to the number of persons entering the pool.
  • Guests will be provided pool towels which are sanitized and packed in individual packaging
  • Pool towels and sunbeds will be sanitized after each use. Guests are encouraged to drop off used towels at the designated place.
  • Sunbeds will be arranged in-keeping with the safe social distancing standard which is one meter.
  • Chlorination and area disinfection of the pool will take place at regular intervals.
Gym
  • The gym may only be utilized by prior appointment.
  • All equipment will be sanitized before and after every use.
  • Towels will be laundered after each use.
  • Guests will be encouraged to sanitize their hands before and after using gym equipment.
  • A mat for sanitizing footwear will be placed at the entrance to the gym.
Indoor/Outdoor Games
  • The gym may only be utilized by prior appointment.
  • All equipment will be sanitized before and after every use.
  • Towels will be laundered after each use.
  • Guests will be encouraged to sanitize their hands before and after using gym equipment.







SUSTAINABILITY/ ENVIRONMENT POLICY

We believe in a greener tomorrow. That is why, as one of the leading hill country hotels in Sri Lanka, we actively commit towards environmental conservation. Mahaweli Reach Hotel continually strives to minimise its carbon footprint making as little impact as possible on the environment. As part of this initiative, we have joined hands together with local communities to support environmentally friendly projects as well as find sustainable ways to give back to Mother Earth.

Having recognised that hotel costs can be reduced and efficiency increased through investing in innovative environmental technologies and improved operational procedures, we implemented a number of key strategies. These included steps to reduce, reuse and recycle waste, effective water management, sustainable procurement and employing energy saving practices. In addition to this, we look to continually hold training programmes for our staff which help to improve employee performance and satisfaction.

As part of our ongoing green initiatives, Mahaweli Reach Hotel has also signed up with the Carbon Consulting Company. The company specialises in offering sustainable business solutions such as Carbon & Water Footprinting, Energy Auditing, Resource Efficient Cleaner Production with Material Auditing and Life Cycle Analysis.

The hotel is in the process of obtaining carbon neutral certification following a stringent process which takes a total of three years and covers carbon, water & waste management. Comprehensive training and audits are periodically carried out as well as part of achieving our goal and the necessary certification.
